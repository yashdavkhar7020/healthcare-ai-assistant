# Patient Discharge Instructions and Post-Hospital Care Policy

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this document is to provide standardized discharge instructions for patients leaving ABC Multi-Speciality Hospital after inpatient treatment, surgery, observation, or emergency care. These guidelines are intended to promote a safe recovery, reduce the risk of complications, prevent avoidable hospital readmissions, and ensure continuity of care after discharge.

Every patient receives discharge instructions tailored to their medical condition. Patients and caregivers are encouraged to ask questions before leaving the hospital to ensure they understand the recommended care plan.

---

# 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

* Adult patients
* Pediatric patients (through parents or legal guardians)
* Surgical patients
* Medical inpatients
* Observation patients
* Patients discharged after emergency treatment
* Patients discharged following telehealth-directed hospital admission

This policy does not replace instructions provided by the treating physician for specific medical conditions.

---

# 3. Discharge Eligibility

A patient may be discharged when the treating physician determines that:

* The patient's medical condition is stable.
* Hospital-level care is no longer required.
* Necessary treatments have been completed.
* Appropriate follow-up arrangements have been made.
* The patient or caregiver understands the discharge instructions.

In certain cases, discharge may be delayed until transportation, home care services, or rehabilitation arrangements have been confirmed.

---

# 4. Before Leaving the Hospital

Before discharge, patients should ensure they receive:

* A discharge summary
* Prescription medications or prescriptions
* Medication instructions
* Follow-up appointment information
* Laboratory or imaging reports if applicable
* Referral letters when required
* Medical certificates if requested
* Emergency contact information

Patients should carefully review all documents before leaving the hospital.

---

# 5. Medication Instructions

Patients must take all prescribed medications exactly as instructed by their healthcare provider.

Medication instructions should include:

* Medication name
* Dosage
* Frequency
* Duration of treatment
* Special instructions
* Possible side effects

Patients should:

* Complete the full course of prescribed medications unless instructed otherwise.
* Avoid skipping doses.
* Never increase or decrease medication dosage without consulting a healthcare provider.
* Inform their physician immediately if they experience serious side effects or allergic reactions.

Patients who require medication refills after discharge may request eligible refills through scheduled follow-up appointments or approved telehealth consultations in accordance with the hospital's Medication Refill Policy.

---

# 6. Activity Guidelines

Recovery recommendations vary depending on the patient's condition and treatment.

Patients are generally advised to:

* Get adequate rest.
* Gradually return to normal daily activities.
* Avoid strenuous physical activity until medically cleared.
* Follow lifting restrictions if applicable.
* Avoid driving while taking medications that may cause drowsiness.
* Perform prescribed rehabilitation exercises if instructed.

Patients recovering from surgery should avoid activities that may place excessive strain on the surgical site.

---

# 7. Diet and Hydration

Patients should follow dietary recommendations provided by their healthcare team.

General recommendations include:

* Drink adequate fluids unless medically restricted.
* Eat balanced meals rich in fruits, vegetables, lean protein, and whole grains.
* Limit excessive salt intake when advised.
* Reduce consumption of processed foods.
* Follow diabetic, cardiac, renal, or other specialized diets when prescribed.

Patients experiencing nausea, vomiting, or difficulty eating should contact their healthcare provider.

---

# 8. Wound Care Instructions

Patients with surgical wounds or injuries should:

* Keep the wound clean and dry.
* Change dressings according to medical instructions.
* Wash hands before touching the wound.
* Monitor for signs of infection.
* Avoid applying unapproved creams or medications.

Patients should immediately report:

* Increasing redness
* Swelling
* Pus or unusual drainage
* Persistent bleeding
* Foul odor
* Fever associated with wound infection

---

# 9. Follow-Up Appointments

Follow-up appointments are an important part of recovery.

Patients should attend all scheduled follow-up visits even if they feel well.

Follow-up appointments may include:

* Physician review
* Laboratory testing
* Imaging studies
* Medication review
* Physical therapy
* Specialist consultation

Some follow-up consultations may be conducted through telehealth when considered clinically appropriate by the treating physician.

---

# 10. Warning Signs Requiring Immediate Medical Attention

Patients should seek immediate medical care if they experience:

* Chest pain
* Difficulty breathing
* Severe abdominal pain
* Uncontrolled bleeding
* Loss of consciousness
* Sudden weakness or numbness
* Persistent high fever
* Severe allergic reactions
* Continuous vomiting
* Confusion or altered mental status

These symptoms may indicate serious complications requiring urgent evaluation.

---

# 11. Home Monitoring

Depending on the patient's condition, healthcare providers may recommend monitoring:

* Blood pressure
* Blood sugar levels
* Body temperature
* Body weight
* Oxygen saturation
* Heart rate

Patients should maintain a record of these measurements if instructed and bring the information to follow-up appointments.

---

# 12. Pain Management

Some discomfort during recovery may be expected.

Patients should:

* Take pain medication only as prescribed.
* Avoid exceeding recommended doses.
* Inform their physician if pain becomes severe or progressively worsens.
* Avoid alcohol while taking prescribed pain medications unless approved by a healthcare provider.

Patients should not use leftover medications from previous illnesses without medical advice.

---

# 13. Returning to Work or School

The timing of returning to work, school, or normal daily activities depends on:

* Medical condition
* Type of treatment received
* Surgical recovery
* Physician recommendations
* Physical demands of daily activities

Patients should obtain medical clearance when required.

Medical certificates may be provided upon request.

---

# 14. Transportation After Discharge

Some patients may not be able to drive safely after discharge due to:

* Recent surgery
* Sedation
* Pain medications
* Physical limitations

Patients are encouraged to arrange transportation with a responsible family member, caregiver, or authorized transportation service.

Healthcare providers may recommend avoiding driving for a specified period.

---

# 15. Caregiver Responsibilities

Family members or caregivers play an important role during recovery.

Caregivers should:

* Understand medication schedules.
* Assist with mobility if necessary.
* Monitor for warning signs.
* Encourage adequate nutrition and hydration.
* Help schedule follow-up appointments.
* Contact healthcare providers if concerns arise.

Caregivers should also ensure that the patient's discharge instructions are followed carefully.

---

# 16. Telehealth Follow-Up

For eligible patients, follow-up care may be conducted through telehealth consultations.

Telehealth follow-up visits are appropriate for:

* Recovery assessment
* Medication review
* Discussion of laboratory results
* Monitoring chronic medical conditions
* General recovery questions

Healthcare providers may recommend an in-person appointment if additional evaluation becomes necessary.

---

# 17. Preventing Hospital Readmission

Patients can reduce the risk of readmission by:

* Taking medications correctly.
* Attending follow-up appointments.
* Following dietary recommendations.
* Remaining physically active within physician-approved limits.
* Monitoring symptoms.
* Reporting new or worsening symptoms promptly.
* Maintaining communication with healthcare providers.

Early identification of complications often improves treatment outcomes.

---

# 18. Patient Responsibilities

Following discharge, patients are responsible for:

* Following all discharge instructions.
* Taking medications as prescribed.
* Attending scheduled appointments.
* Maintaining updated contact information.
* Informing healthcare providers about changes in symptoms.
* Seeking emergency care when appropriate.

Patients should never ignore symptoms that appear to be worsening.

---

# 19. Hospital Responsibilities

ABC Multi-Speciality Hospital is committed to supporting patients after discharge by:

* Providing clear written discharge instructions.
* Explaining medication changes.
* Scheduling appropriate follow-up appointments.
* Coordinating referrals when necessary.
* Protecting patient privacy.
* Offering telehealth follow-up services when appropriate.
* Responding to patient questions regarding discharge instructions.

Hospital staff will make reasonable efforts to ensure patients understand their care plan before discharge.

---

# 20. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. Can I request a telehealth follow-up appointment after discharge?**
Yes. Patients with eligible medical conditions may attend follow-up appointments through telehealth if the treating physician determines that an in-person examination is not required.

**Q2. What should I do if I forget how to take my medication?**
Contact your healthcare provider or hospital pharmacy before making any changes to your medication schedule.

**Q3. Can I resume normal physical activity immediately?**
No. Patients should gradually return to normal activities and follow any restrictions provided by their physician.

**Q4. When should I seek emergency medical care?**
Seek immediate medical attention if you experience chest pain, difficulty breathing, severe bleeding, loss of consciousness, persistent high fever, severe allergic reactions, or any rapidly worsening symptoms.

**Q5. Can I request medication refills after discharge?**
Yes. Eligible refill requests may be submitted through follow-up appointments or approved telehealth consultations according to the hospital's Medication Refill Policy.

**Q6. What if my wound becomes red or swollen?**
Increasing redness, swelling, drainage, fever, or persistent pain may indicate infection. Contact your healthcare provider promptly for evaluation.

**Q7. Why are follow-up appointments important?**
Follow-up visits allow healthcare providers to monitor recovery, review medications, evaluate treatment progress, identify complications early, and make adjustments to your care plan if necessary.
