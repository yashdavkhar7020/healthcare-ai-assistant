# Telehealth Consultation Policy

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this Telehealth Consultation Policy is to establish standardized guidelines for delivering safe, secure, and effective healthcare services through virtual consultations at ABC Multi-Speciality Hospital. Telehealth services are intended to improve access to healthcare, reduce unnecessary travel, and support continuity of care while maintaining high standards of patient safety, privacy, and clinical quality.

This policy outlines patient eligibility, appointment procedures, technology requirements, provider responsibilities, limitations of telehealth, medication refill guidelines, and privacy practices.

---

# 2. Scope

This policy applies to all healthcare professionals, administrative staff, and patients participating in telehealth consultations provided by ABC Multi-Speciality Hospital.

Telehealth services may include:

* Primary care consultations
* Follow-up appointments
* Chronic disease management
* Medication review
* Preventive healthcare consultations
* Mental health counseling
* Nutritional counseling
* Laboratory result discussions
* Post-discharge follow-up visits

Emergency medical care is not included within the scope of telehealth services.

---

# 3. Eligibility for Telehealth Services

Patients may be eligible for telehealth consultations if their medical condition can be reasonably evaluated without requiring an immediate physical examination.

Examples of appropriate telehealth consultations include:

* Follow-up visits after treatment
* Stable chronic disease management
* Prescription renewals for eligible medications
* Review of laboratory reports
* Minor illnesses with non-emergency symptoms
* Lifestyle and wellness counseling
* Medication side-effect discussions
* General health education

The attending healthcare provider will determine whether telehealth is clinically appropriate for each patient.

---

# 4. Situations Where Telehealth Is Not Appropriate

Telehealth consultations should not be used for medical emergencies or situations requiring immediate in-person assessment.

Patients should seek emergency medical care immediately if they experience symptoms such as:

* Chest pain
* Severe difficulty breathing
* Sudden loss of consciousness
* Stroke symptoms
* Severe allergic reactions
* Major injuries or trauma
* Heavy bleeding
* Seizures
* Sudden vision loss
* Suicidal thoughts or psychiatric emergencies

Hospital staff may advise patients to visit the Emergency Department if urgent medical evaluation is necessary.

---

# 5. Appointment Scheduling

Telehealth appointments may be scheduled through:

* Hospital website
* Mobile application
* Call center
* Hospital reception desk

Patients should provide:

* Full name
* Contact information
* Patient identification number (if available)
* Reason for consultation
* Preferred physician or department
* Insurance information when applicable

Appointments are considered confirmed only after the patient receives an official confirmation from the hospital.

---

# 6. Technology Requirements

Patients are responsible for ensuring that they have appropriate technology before the scheduled consultation.

Minimum requirements include:

* Smartphone, tablet, laptop, or desktop computer
* Camera and microphone
* Stable internet connection
* Updated web browser or hospital application
* Quiet and private location

Patients are encouraged to test their device before the consultation to minimize technical issues.

---

# 7. Patient Check-In

Patients should join the virtual consultation approximately **10 minutes before** the scheduled appointment.

Before the consultation begins, patients may be asked to:

* Verify their identity
* Confirm contact information
* Review consent for telehealth services
* Update medication information
* Confirm current location for emergency purposes if required

Late arrival may reduce consultation time or require rescheduling depending on physician availability.

---

# 8. Identity Verification

To protect patient privacy and ensure accurate medical records, healthcare providers may request identity verification before beginning the consultation.

Acceptable verification may include:

* Patient name
* Date of birth
* Registered phone number
* Government-issued identification when necessary

Healthcare providers may refuse to continue the consultation if patient identity cannot be reasonably confirmed.

---

# 9. Patient Consent

Participation in telehealth services requires informed patient consent.

Before the consultation, patients acknowledge that:

* Telehealth has limitations compared to in-person visits.
* Technical interruptions may occur.
* Some medical conditions cannot be fully evaluated remotely.
* Physicians may recommend an in-person appointment when clinically appropriate.

Consent may be obtained electronically or verbally and documented in the patient's medical record.

---

# 10. Medication Refill Policy

Patients may request medication refills during telehealth consultations under appropriate clinical circumstances.

Medication refill requests may be approved when:

* The medication has been previously prescribed by an authorized healthcare provider.
* The patient's condition remains clinically stable.
* The physician determines that an in-person examination is not required.
* No signs indicate worsening illness or medication misuse.

Medication refill requests may be denied if:

* The medication requires physical examination before renewal.
* Additional laboratory testing is required.
* The medication is classified as a controlled substance requiring in-person evaluation.
* The physician believes further assessment is necessary for patient safety.

Patients should request refills before their medication supply is exhausted to avoid treatment interruptions.

---

# 11. Laboratory Results

Telehealth consultations are appropriate for discussing laboratory and diagnostic test results.

Healthcare providers may:

* Explain laboratory findings.
* Recommend lifestyle modifications.
* Adjust treatment plans.
* Order additional investigations.
* Recommend specialist referrals when appropriate.

Patients requiring physical examination following abnormal test results may be advised to schedule an in-person visit.

---

# 12. Clinical Documentation

Healthcare providers will document telehealth consultations in the patient's medical record using the same professional standards applied to in-person visits.

Documentation typically includes:

* Consultation date and time
* Patient identity verification
* Chief complaint
* Medical history
* Assessment
* Treatment recommendations
* Follow-up plan
* Technical issues encountered during the consultation

Clinical documentation becomes part of the patient's permanent medical record.

---

# 13. Privacy and Confidentiality

ABC Multi-Speciality Hospital is committed to protecting patient privacy during telehealth consultations.

Healthcare providers will:

* Conduct consultations using approved secure communication platforms.
* Access only necessary patient information.
* Maintain confidentiality of all discussions.
* Avoid recording consultations unless specifically authorized by hospital policy and patient consent.

Patients are encouraged to participate from a private location where confidential medical discussions cannot be overheard.

---

# 14. Technical Interruptions

Occasionally, technical difficulties may interrupt telehealth consultations.

Examples include:

* Internet connectivity issues
* Audio failure
* Video failure
* Platform outages
* Device malfunction

If the consultation is interrupted, reasonable efforts will be made to reconnect.

If reconnection is unsuccessful, hospital staff may:

* Continue the consultation by telephone if clinically appropriate.
* Reschedule the appointment.
* Recommend an in-person consultation if necessary.

---

# 15. Physician Responsibilities

Healthcare providers participating in telehealth consultations are responsible for:

* Reviewing relevant medical records.
* Verifying patient identity.
* Determining clinical suitability for telehealth.
* Maintaining professional communication.
* Protecting patient confidentiality.
* Documenting the consultation accurately.
* Referring patients for in-person care when appropriate.

Physicians should avoid making clinical decisions when insufficient information is available through virtual assessment.

---

# 16. Patient Responsibilities

Patients participating in telehealth services are expected to:

* Join appointments on time.
* Provide accurate medical information.
* Inform the provider of current medications.
* Participate from a safe and private environment.
* Ensure adequate internet connectivity.
* Follow medical advice provided during the consultation.
* Seek emergency care when advised.

Patients should avoid participating while driving or engaging in activities that could interfere with effective communication.

---

# 17. Follow-Up Care

Following a telehealth consultation, healthcare providers may recommend:

* Home care instructions
* Prescription medications
* Additional laboratory tests
* Imaging studies
* Specialist referrals
* In-person evaluation
* Follow-up telehealth consultation

Patients should follow the recommended care plan and contact the hospital if symptoms worsen before the scheduled follow-up.

---

# 18. Limitations of Telehealth

While telehealth improves healthcare accessibility, it cannot replace every aspect of traditional medical care.

Limitations include:

* Inability to perform physical examination
* Limited diagnostic capabilities
* Dependence on technology
* Potential communication challenges
* Variable internet quality
* Difficulty assessing certain medical conditions remotely

Healthcare providers may discontinue the virtual consultation and recommend an in-person visit whenever remote assessment is considered insufficient.

---

# 19. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. Can I request medication refills during a telehealth consultation?**
Yes. Patients may request medication refills for medications that have been previously prescribed, provided the treating physician determines that an in-person examination is not required and the medication is appropriate for remote renewal.

**Q2. Can telehealth be used for emergencies?**
No. Telehealth should never replace emergency medical services. Patients experiencing serious or life-threatening symptoms should seek immediate emergency care.

**Q3. What equipment do I need?**
A smartphone, tablet, laptop, or desktop computer with a camera, microphone, and reliable internet connection.

**Q4. Will my consultation remain confidential?**
Yes. The hospital uses secure communication platforms and follows established privacy and confidentiality standards.

**Q5. What happens if my internet connection is interrupted?**
Hospital staff will attempt to reconnect. If unsuccessful, the consultation may continue by telephone when appropriate or be rescheduled.

**Q6. Can my physician ask me to visit the hospital after a telehealth consultation?**
Yes. If a physical examination or additional testing is necessary, the physician may recommend an in-person appointment.
