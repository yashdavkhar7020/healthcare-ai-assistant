# Appointment Scheduling Policy

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this Appointment Scheduling Policy is to establish a standardized process for scheduling, rescheduling, confirming, and cancelling patient appointments at ABC Multi-Speciality Hospital. The policy is designed to ensure timely access to healthcare services, efficient utilization of hospital resources, and a consistent patient experience. It applies to all outpatient departments, specialty clinics, telehealth consultations, diagnostic services, and follow-up visits.

This policy aims to reduce appointment delays, improve patient satisfaction, optimize physician schedules, and minimize missed appointments while maintaining fairness and transparency in appointment allocation.

---

# 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

* New patient appointments
* Returning patient follow-up appointments
* Specialist consultations
* Diagnostic and laboratory appointments
* Telehealth consultations
* Preventive health check-up appointments
* Referral-based appointments

Emergency medical care is not covered under this policy. Patients experiencing medical emergencies should immediately contact emergency services or visit the Emergency Department.

---

# 3. Appointment Booking Methods

Patients may schedule appointments through any of the following channels:

* Hospital reception desk during operating hours
* Hospital call center
* Official hospital website
* Mobile application
* Authorized patient support representatives

Appointment requests submitted through digital platforms are subject to confirmation by the scheduling system or hospital staff.

---

# 4. Information Required for Booking

Patients are expected to provide accurate information when scheduling an appointment. The following details are generally required:

* Full name
* Date of birth
* Contact number
* Email address (optional)
* Preferred department or physician
* Reason for visit
* Existing patient identification number (if applicable)
* Insurance information (if applicable)

Providing inaccurate or incomplete information may delay appointment confirmation.

---

# 5. Appointment Confirmation

Appointments are considered confirmed only after the patient receives confirmation through one of the following methods:

* SMS confirmation
* Email confirmation
* Mobile application notification
* Confirmation by hospital staff over the phone

The confirmation message will typically include:

* Appointment ID
* Department
* Physician name
* Appointment date
* Appointment time
* Hospital location
* Special preparation instructions, if required

Patients are encouraged to review these details carefully and contact the hospital immediately if any corrections are necessary.

---

# 6. Arrival Guidelines

Patients should arrive at least **15 minutes before** their scheduled appointment time.

This additional time allows for:

* Identity verification
* Insurance verification
* Registration updates
* Completion of required forms
* Payment processing, if applicable

Patients arriving significantly late may be requested to reschedule depending on physician availability.

---

# 7. Late Arrival Policy

If a patient expects to arrive late, they should notify the hospital as early as possible.

The following general guidelines apply:

* Arriving within 10 minutes of the scheduled appointment usually allows the consultation to proceed.
* Arrivals exceeding 15 minutes may result in reduced consultation time.
* Arrivals exceeding 20 minutes may require appointment rescheduling based on physician discretion and schedule availability.

Hospital staff will make reasonable efforts to accommodate delayed patients whenever possible without negatively affecting other scheduled appointments.

---

# 8. Appointment Rescheduling

Patients may reschedule appointments if they are unable to attend.

Rescheduling requests should preferably be made at least **24 hours** before the scheduled appointment.

Appointments may be rescheduled through:

* Call center
* Website
* Mobile application
* Hospital reception

Repeated last-minute rescheduling may reduce appointment priority for high-demand specialists.

---

# 9. Appointment Cancellation

Patients should cancel appointments as soon as they become aware they cannot attend.

Advance cancellations help make appointment slots available for other patients.

Cancellation can be completed through:

* Online patient portal
* Mobile application
* Telephone support
* Reception desk

No cancellation fee is charged for appointments cancelled at least 24 hours in advance.

Certain specialized diagnostic procedures may have separate cancellation policies.

---

# 10. Missed Appointments

A patient who neither attends nor cancels an appointment is considered a "No-Show."

Repeated missed appointments may result in:

* Reduced scheduling priority
* Requirement for telephone confirmation before future bookings
* Longer waiting periods for specialist appointments

The hospital may contact patients after repeated missed appointments to understand any barriers to care and provide scheduling assistance.

---

# 11. Appointment Reminders

To reduce missed appointments, reminder notifications are automatically sent.

Patients may receive reminders:

* Seven days before the appointment
* Twenty-four hours before the appointment
* Two hours before the appointment (optional)

Reminder notifications do not replace the patient's responsibility to attend or cancel appointments.

---

# 12. Specialist Referrals

Certain specialist consultations require a physician referral.

Examples include:

* Cardiology
* Neurology
* Oncology
* Rheumatology

Referral documentation should be submitted before the appointment whenever possible to avoid delays.

Patients without required referrals may need to reschedule unless hospital policy allows direct specialist access.

---

# 13. Telehealth Appointments

Eligible patients may schedule telehealth consultations for appropriate medical conditions.

Telehealth appointments are generally suitable for:

* Routine follow-up visits
* Medication review
* Chronic disease management
* Minor illness consultations
* Laboratory result discussion

Telehealth appointments are generally **not appropriate** for medical emergencies, severe injuries, or situations requiring physical examination.

Patients are responsible for ensuring they have:

* Stable internet connection
* Camera-enabled device
* Quiet and private environment

Patients should log in approximately 10 minutes before the scheduled consultation.

---

# 14. Diagnostic Appointments

Some diagnostic appointments require preparation before arrival.

Examples include:

* Fasting before blood tests
* Drinking water before ultrasound
* Medication adjustments before imaging procedures

Preparation instructions are included in the appointment confirmation whenever applicable.

Failure to follow preparation instructions may require appointment rescheduling.

---

# 15. Insurance Verification

Patients using health insurance should provide valid insurance information during appointment booking.

Insurance eligibility may be verified before the appointment.

If insurance cannot be verified, patients may be responsible for payment until coverage is confirmed.

Coverage approval remains subject to the policies of the insurance provider.

---

# 16. Pediatric Appointments

Children under 18 years of age should attend appointments with a parent, legal guardian, or authorized caregiver.

The accompanying adult may be required to:

* Present identification
* Complete consent forms
* Participate in medical discussions

Additional consent may be required for specific procedures.

---

# 17. Accessibility Services

ABC Multi-Speciality Hospital is committed to providing accessible healthcare services.

Patients requiring additional assistance may request:

* Wheelchair support
* Language interpretation
* Hearing assistance
* Mobility assistance
* Extended appointment duration for accessibility needs

Requests should ideally be communicated during appointment booking.

---

# 18. Patient Responsibilities

Patients are expected to:

* Provide accurate personal information.
* Bring identification documents.
* Carry insurance information when applicable.
* Inform staff about appointment changes promptly.
* Arrive on time.
* Follow preparation instructions.
* Treat hospital staff respectfully.

Patients should notify the hospital if their contact information changes to ensure appointment communications are received.

---

# 19. Hospital Responsibilities

The hospital is responsible for:

* Providing reasonable appointment availability.
* Protecting patient privacy.
* Sending appointment confirmations and reminders.
* Maintaining accurate scheduling records.
* Informing patients about significant delays whenever possible.
* Offering rescheduling options when physicians become unavailable.

Every effort will be made to minimize waiting times while recognizing that emergency medical situations may occasionally affect clinic schedules.

---

# 20. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. Can I schedule appointments online?**
Yes. Appointments can be requested through the hospital website and mobile application.

**Q2. Can I reschedule my appointment?**
Yes. Patients are encouraged to request rescheduling at least 24 hours before the appointment.

**Q3. What happens if I arrive late?**
Late arrivals may still be accommodated depending on physician availability. Significant delays may require rescheduling.

**Q4. Can someone else book an appointment for me?**
Yes, provided they have the necessary patient information and authorization where applicable.

**Q5. Will I receive appointment reminders?**
Yes. Reminder notifications are generally sent seven days, twenty-four hours, and optionally two hours before the appointment.

**Q6. Can I book a telehealth consultation?**
Yes. Telehealth appointments are available for eligible medical conditions and follow-up care.

**Q7. Do I need a referral for every specialist?**
Not always. Some specialties require physician referrals, while others may allow direct appointments depending on hospital policy.
