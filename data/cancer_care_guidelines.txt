# Cancer Care Guidelines

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this document is to establish general guidelines for cancer screening, diagnosis, treatment coordination, patient education, follow-up care, and supportive services provided by the Oncology Department of ABC Multi-Speciality Hospital. These guidelines promote high-quality, patient-centered care while ensuring safety, continuity, and informed decision-making throughout the treatment journey.

## 2. Scope

These guidelines apply to all patients receiving cancer-related consultations, diagnostic evaluations, chemotherapy, radiation therapy referrals, surgical oncology services, survivorship care, and palliative care consultations.

## 3. Initial Evaluation

Patients referred to the Oncology Department undergo a comprehensive clinical evaluation. This includes reviewing the patient's medical history, family history, laboratory investigations, pathology reports, imaging studies, previous treatments, allergies, and current medications.

Additional diagnostic tests may be ordered when necessary to confirm the diagnosis or determine the stage of disease.

## 4. Cancer Screening

Routine cancer screening is recommended according to age, family history, medical history, and physician recommendations.

Examples include:

- Breast cancer screening using mammography.
- Cervical cancer screening using Pap tests.
- Colorectal cancer screening through colonoscopy or stool-based tests.
- Lung cancer screening for eligible high-risk individuals.
- Prostate cancer screening after discussion with a healthcare provider.

Screening recommendations should always be individualized.

## 5. Treatment Planning

Every patient receives an individualized treatment plan developed by a multidisciplinary team. The team may include:

- Medical oncologists
- Surgical oncologists
- Radiation oncologists
- Pathologists
- Radiologists
- Oncology nurses
- Dietitians
- Psychologists
- Social workers

Treatment recommendations depend on cancer type, stage, patient preferences, overall health, and expected outcomes.

## 6. Chemotherapy

Chemotherapy medications are prescribed only after appropriate evaluation by an oncologist.

Before each chemotherapy cycle, patients may undergo blood tests to ensure treatment can be safely administered.

Patients should immediately report symptoms including:

- Fever
- Persistent vomiting
- Severe diarrhea
- Uncontrolled pain
- Difficulty breathing
- Chest pain
- Excessive bleeding

Dose adjustments may be required depending on treatment response and side effects.

## 7. Radiation Therapy

Radiation therapy may be recommended alone or together with surgery or chemotherapy.

Treatment schedules vary depending on the diagnosis.

Patients should attend every scheduled radiation session whenever possible.

Common temporary side effects include fatigue, mild skin irritation, and localized discomfort.

## 8. Surgical Oncology

Some cancers require surgical removal of tumors before or after additional therapies.

Patients receive pre-operative counseling regarding:

- Procedure details
- Expected recovery
- Possible risks
- Hospital stay
- Follow-up appointments

Recovery plans differ according to the type of surgery performed.

## 9. Follow-up Care

Regular follow-up visits help monitor recovery, identify recurrence, evaluate treatment response, and manage long-term side effects.

Patients are encouraged to attend every scheduled follow-up appointment even if they feel well.

Typical follow-up may include:

- Physical examinations
- Blood investigations
- Imaging studies
- Medication review
- Symptom assessment

## 10. Supportive Care

Cancer treatment may affect emotional, nutritional, physical, and psychological well-being.

Supportive care services available include:

- Nutrition counseling
- Pain management
- Psychological counseling
- Rehabilitation services
- Financial counseling
- Social work support

Patients and family members are encouraged to seek assistance whenever needed.

## 11. Palliative Care

Palliative care focuses on improving quality of life by managing symptoms, reducing discomfort, and supporting patients and families.

Palliative care is appropriate at any stage of serious illness and may be provided alongside active cancer treatment.

Services may include pain control, emotional support, symptom management, and advance care planning.

## 12. Patient Responsibilities

Patients should:

- Attend scheduled appointments.
- Follow prescribed treatment plans.
- Inform healthcare providers of new symptoms.
- Maintain an updated medication list.
- Report side effects promptly.
- Discuss concerns openly with the healthcare team.

Patients should never stop cancer medications without consulting their treating physician.

## 13. Infection Prevention During Treatment

Cancer treatments may weaken the immune system.

Patients should:

- Wash hands frequently.
- Avoid close contact with individuals who are ill.
- Monitor body temperature daily if instructed.
- Seek medical attention immediately for fever above 38°C.
- Maintain good oral hygiene.

Vaccination recommendations should always be discussed with the oncology team.

## 14. Emergency Situations

Patients should seek immediate medical care if they experience:

- Severe chest pain
- Difficulty breathing
- Uncontrolled bleeding
- Loss of consciousness
- Persistent high fever
- Severe allergic reactions
- Sudden confusion

Emergency services should be contacted whenever urgent medical attention is required.

## 15. Documentation

All consultations, investigations, treatment decisions, medication changes, informed consent discussions, and follow-up recommendations are documented in the patient's electronic health record.

## 16. Review

These guidelines are reviewed periodically to ensure compliance with current oncology standards, hospital policies, and evolving clinical best practices.
